================================================================================
DIGITAL DETOX DAY: SIMULATION REPORT
================================================================================

SCENARIO:
  On Day 10, ALL 100 agents reduce their social media usage by 50%
  (vs. original intervention: only 5 influencers reduce usage)

RESEARCH QUESTION:
  Does a collective network-wide 'digital detox day' produce better
  outcomes than targeted intervention on high-degree influencers?

METHODOLOGY:
  - 100 agents with same network topology (seed=42)
  - 5 simulation runs (seeds 42-46)
  - Day 10: ALL agents reduce app_usage_time by 50%
  - No transmission dampening (agents still influence each other)

================================================================================
RESULTS
================================================================================

Final Network Stress: 30.20 ± 0.88
Burnout Count:        0.60 ± 0.55
Total Stress AUC:     83205 ± 1021

================================================================================
COMPARISON WITH OTHER SCENARIOS
================================================================================

Scenario                       Final Stress    Burnout Count  
------------------------------ --------------- ---------------
Baseline (no intervention)      42.61           5.80
Target 5 Influencers            40.09           5.40
Digital Detox Day (ALL 100)     30.20           0.60

IMPROVEMENT ANALYSIS:

Digital Detox Day vs Baseline:
  Stress reduction:     +29.1%
  Burnout reduction:    +5.20 cases

Digital Detox Day vs Targeted Intervention (5 influencers):
  Stress difference:    +24.7%
  Burnout difference:   +4.80 cases

================================================================================
CONCLUSION
================================================================================

✓ DIGITAL DETOX DAY IS MORE EFFECTIVE

  A network-wide intervention (100 agents @ 50% reduction) produces
  better outcomes than targeted intervention (5 influencers).

  IMPLICATION: Collective action beats targeted intervention.
               Mobilizing the entire network is more effective than
               relying on influencer-driven cascades.

================================================================================
TARGETING STRATEGY COMPARISON REPORT
================================================================================

RESEARCH QUESTION:
  Does targeting high-degree influencers produce better intervention
  outcomes than targeting random or low-degree agents?

METHODOLOGY:
  - 3 targeting strategies tested (Top 5, Random 5, Bottom 5)
  - 5 simulation runs per strategy (seeds 42-46)
  - Same network topology and agents across all runs
  - Intervention: 50% usage cut + 70% transmission dampening at Day 10

================================================================================
CONCLUSION
================================================================================

✓ TARGETING INFLUENCERS IS SIGNIFICANTLY MORE EFFECTIVE

  Network position matters for intervention effectiveness.
  High-degree nodes (influencers) have amplified impact on network stress.
  Targeted intervention is superior to random or low-degree targeting.

  IMPLICATION: The simulation demonstrates that network-aware interventions
               can achieve better outcomes with the same resource investment
               (5 agents). This validates the core hypothesis.
